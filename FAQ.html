General Tipping Questions

What is the standard tip percentage at restaurants?

The standard tip at restaurants in the United States is 15-20% of the pre-tax bill. For good service, 18-20% is appropriate. Exceptional service warrants 20% or more. If service was poor, 10-15% is acceptable, though you should speak with management about serious issues.

Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax amount?

Traditionally, tips are calculated on the pre-tax amount. However, calculating on the post-tax total (which is typically only 1-2% higher) is also common and acceptable. The difference is usually negligible, and many people find it easier to tip on the final total shown on their receipt.

Do I need to tip on takeout orders?

Tipping on takeout is optional but appreciated. A tip of 10% shows appreciation for the kitchen staff and the person who packaged your order. For large or complex takeout orders, consider tipping 15%. However, unlike dine-in service, there's no strong obligation to tip on takeout.

How much should I tip for food delivery?

Food delivery typically warrants a 15-20% tip, with a minimum of $3-5 regardless of order size. During bad weather, for long distances, or large orders, consider tipping 20-25%. Remember that delivery drivers often use their own vehicles and pay for gas, making tips a significant part of their income.

Is it okay to tip less for bad service?

Yes, it's acceptable to reduce your tip for genuinely poor service, but consider the circumstances first. If the restaurant was understaffed or the kitchen was slow, these issues aren't your server's fault. If the poor service was directly due to your server's attitude or inattention, tipping 10-15% and speaking with management is appropriate.

Should I tip if a service charge is already included?

If a service charge or automatic gratuity is already included in your bill (common for large parties), you're not obligated to tip additionally. However, if service was exceptional, adding 5-10% on top of the service charge is a generous gesture that will be appreciated.

Bill Splitting Questions

What's the fairest way to split a bill?

The fairest method depends on your group and situation. For friends who regularly dine together and order similarly, splitting equally is easiest. For groups where people ordered very different amounts, itemized splitting ensures everyone pays for what they ordered. The key is establishing expectations before ordering.

How do I handle splitting when some people drank alcohol and others didn't?

Separate alcohol costs from food costs. Split the food bill according to your normal method (equally or itemized), then have those who ordered alcohol split those costs among themselves. This approach is fair and prevents non-drinkers from subsidizing expensive drinks they didn't consume.

Should the tip be split equally even if the bill isn't?

When splitting a bill itemized, each person's tip should be proportional to their subtotal. If you ordered $30 worth of food and someone else ordered $60, they should pay twice as much in tip as well. Our calculator handles this automatically when you use the bill splitting feature.

How do I split the bill for a birthday celebration?

For birthday celebrations, it's customary for guests to split the birthday person's portion. Calculate the total bill, subtract the honoree's meal cost, then divide the remaining amount among the other diners. This way, everyone contributes to treating the guest of honor.

What if someone orders much more expensive items than everyone else?

Special Situations

Should I tip at coffee shops with counter service?

Tipping at coffee shops is appreciated but not mandatory like at sit-down restaurants. If there's a tip jar, adding $0.50-$1 for simple orders or 10-15% for complex drinks is generous. Regular customers often tip more consistently to build rapport with baristas.

How much should I tip for hotel housekeeping?

Tip hotel housekeeping $2-5 per night, left daily rather than at checkout since different staff may service your room each day. Leave the tip in a visible location with a note indicating it's for housekeeping to ensure it reaches the right person.

What about tipping at buffets?

At buffets where you serve yourself, tipping 10% is standard since service is more limited. If a server brings drinks, clears plates frequently, or provides other attentive service, consider tipping 15%. The reduced tip percentage reflects the reduced service compared to traditional dining.

Should I tip less at expensive restaurants?

No, tip percentage should remain consistent regardless of the restaurant's price point. A $200 meal requires the same percentage tip as a $50 meal. The higher absolute amount naturally reflects the more expensive establishment, but the percentage should stay at 15-20% based on service quality.

How do I tip when using a gift card?

Always tip on the full amount of the meal before the gift card is applied. If your meal costs $60 and you have a $50 gift card, tip on the $60, not the $10 difference you're paying. Servers provided full service regardless of your payment method.

International Tipping

Is tipping expected in other countries?

Tipping customs vary dramatically worldwide. In many European countries, service charges are included in the bill, making additional tipping optional. In some Asian countries, tipping can be considered rude. Always research local customs before traveling to avoid cultural missteps.

How much should I tip when traveling abroad?

When traveling, research tipping customs for your specific destination. In countries where tipping is expected, 10% is often sufficient (compared to 15-20% in the US). In countries where service charges are included, rounding up the bill or leaving small change is a polite gesture but not required.
